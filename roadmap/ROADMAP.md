# Teach Me Claude — The Full Journey

One hundred challenges, ten series. From your first conversation with an
AI agent to shipping real software — each challenge a short lesson plus a
practical task, each series ending with something *installed and running
in your real work*, not just learned.

**Series 1 (Foundations) is free and included in this plugin** — and it
is complete on its own: by its end you own a working personal toolkit
(your first skill, your brief template, your verification checklist, your
delegation map, your agent's memory file, and a machine you commissioned
yourself). Series 2–10 are paid challenge packs: **€20 per series**
(10 challenges) or **€100 for everything**. Same format, same coach,
deeper water.

**The shape of the journey:**

- **Series 1** builds the foundations — on a sampler of the four kinds of
  knowledge work the next four series each take deep.
- **Series 2–5** are the four domains: **Research**, **Analytics**,
  **Documents & Reports**, **Presentations & Communication** — in
  pipeline order, because Series 5 ends by chaining all four on one real
  project. Each domain series opens by *borrowing* an off-the-shelf
  skill, and closes by *graduating your own*.
- **Series 6–7** turn your way of working into infrastructure: repeatable
  workflows, then a skill library you can share as a plugin.
- **Series 8–10** widen the world: version control and your first public
  website, then Claude Code and automation (with real secrets
  management), then real software with real users.

Most challenges take **15–20 minutes**; early ones are shorter (5–10),
capstones and the later building series run longer. Safety is woven
through the whole arc — basics in Series 1, sharing hygiene in Series 7,
secrets in Series 9, a full hardening pass in Series 10 — because it
belongs where it's live, not in a quarantine chapter.

## Series 1 — Foundations *(free, included)*

What an AI agent really is, how to delegate real work to it, how to make
it interrogate *you*, check it, and stay safe — ending with you
commissioning a working machine for your own recurring work.

1. **Meet your AI coworker** *(5–10 min)* — what an LLM agent is, and the three things it isn't
2. **Your first real delegation** *(10–15 min)* — hand over a real document task, end to end
3. **Say what you mean** *(15 min)* — the brief: context, audience, format, success criteria
4. **Make it grill you** *(15–20 min)* — watch the yes-machine agree with a bad idea; then build your first skill — one that interrogates *you* until the brief is complete
5. **Trust, but verify** *(15–20 min)* — catch your colleague confidently wrong; build your verification checklist
6. **Compute, don't guess** *(15–20 min)* — numbers come from machines, not vibes; sort your work into judgment vs rules; your delegation map
7. **Big jobs, small pieces** *(20 min)* — decomposition, checkpoints, and when to start fresh
8. **Give your agent a memory** *(15–20 min)* — your CLAUDE.md: the operating manual you've been writing all series gets a home
9. **Working safely** *(15–20 min)* — sensitive data, folder scope, hidden instructions, the scrub drill
10. **Build your own machine** *(30–60 min, capstone)* — commission a working tool or workflow for your own recurring work, briefed by your own grill-me skill
