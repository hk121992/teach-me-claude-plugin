# Series 1 — Foundations (free)

Ten challenges that take a non-technical professional from "never worked
with an AI agent" to confidently delegating real work to Claude in Cowork —
with the judgment to know what to delegate, how to brief it, and how to
check the result.

**Design notes for the companion**

- Scaffolding fades across the series: Challenge 1 is a worked example the
  learner studies and modifies; 2–3 are guided fix-and-complete exercises;
  4–9 are increasingly independent; 10 is an open capstone the learner
  designs.
- Challenges 3 and 4 are deliberately *failure-first*: the learner watches
  a weak approach fall short before being taught the technique. Let the
  failure happen — don't rescue them early.
- Every challenge produces a real artifact from the learner's own work and
  ends with one reflection question (recorded by the `review` skill).
- Competency tags follow the 4D fluency model: **Delegation** (what to hand
  over), **Description** (how to brief), **Discernment** (how to judge
  output), **Diligence** (responsibility, safety, verification).

**The arc**

| # | Challenge | Concept | Competency |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Meet your AI coworker | What an LLM agent is — and isn't | Discernment |
| 2 | Your first real delegation | Handing over a real task end-to-end | Delegation |
| 3 | Say what you mean | The brief: context, audience, format, success criteria | Description |
| 4 | Trust, but verify | Hallucination and verification habits | Discernment |
| 5 | The right tool for the job | AI vs deterministic — judgment vs rules | Delegation |
| 6 | Build the machine once | Claude writes reusable, deterministic tools | Delegation |
| 7 | Big jobs, small pieces | Decomposition and checkpoints | Description |
| 8 | Give your agent a memory | Context, CLAUDE.md, persistent preferences | Description |
| 9 | Working safely | Privacy, sensitive data, safe-sharing rules | Diligence |
| 10 | Choose your own adventure | Capstone — integrate everything on a goal they define | All four |
